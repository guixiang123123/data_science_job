Jobs in Data Science

Xiang (Brian) Gui, Dallas, TX · (682) 300-8309 · guixiang123123@outlook.com

A prediction system that tells if international workers can successfully find a job, apply for a working visa and get a good salary in data science related field in US. It could be useful for both international applicants who plan to find a job in US and also those companies which want to hire international talents.

The labor datasets were retrieved from the US Department of Labor’s Office of Foreign Labor Certification from 2008 to 2015 which includes about 2 hundreds thousands instances with 30 features including job titile, wage, company, education background, location, time, etc.

Overview of Wage Distribution

"Outliers succeed"

In Tech industry, if a company is willing to hire an employee for a senior position with higher salary, it will be easier for the employee to get issued a working visa in U.S

Wage

"Actual Paid" vs "Prevailing"

To ensure equity for US and non-US workers, companies need to state how much they plans to pay the employee and also state the average amount for the same position called “the prevailing wage.” Company need to guarrantee "Actual Paid" is more than "Prevailing"

Location and Time

"Geographic Distribution"

The job markets are different between east and west coast. The median wages were colored into state and counts were quantified using pie chart.

"Time line"

The time line reflects the H1B application season while wages of data scientist salary show great variance. The median wages were dot plotted and counts were quantified using stream line.
